# Calibration-driven NVFP4 W4A4 error-reduction techniques (2026-06-23)

> 5-axis research workflow (1 axis "scale-search" failed structured-output; AWQ/SmoothQuant partly covered elsewhere).
> Verified against this repo (format.py/quant.py). Informs gap-closing for DINOv2 W4A4 (-2.5pt k-NN).

## Executive Summary

CONTEXT (verified against the repo). The W4A4 path is RTN. `quantize_to_nvfp4()` picks E2M1 codes by fixed nearest-rounding midpoints (format.py `_MID`: 2.5 decides 2-vs-3, 5.0 decides 4-vs-6), always scales each 16-element block's max to FP4's 6 (`block_scale = amax_b/6 * 1/global`), and sets `global = amax/(6*448)` = MAX. SP3 proved percentile global is HARMFUL on NVFP4 (0.945 max vs 0.660 at p99.9 vs 0.408 at p99): the global's only job is to keep per-block E4M3 scales <=448, so shrinking it below true amax saturates high blocks at 448 and clips them. Global-scale calibration is therefore exhausted as a lever. Current skip(2,2) baseline: feature cosine ~0.95, CIFAR-100 k-NN top-1 0.798 vs BF16 0.822 (-2.5 pt); `block_output_cosines` shows monotonic error accumulation across the 48 quantized middle Linears (1.00 -> 0.93, partial recovery after).

KEY INSIGHT. Because the global MUST be max and the per-16 block already localizes dynamic range, the residual error is dominated by E2M1 ROUNDING inside each block (Four Over Six and SOAR both measure E4M3 scale-quant as the SMALLER error term). So the high-value levers are all INSIDE the block: (a) per-block 6-vs-4 max selection, (b) learned / Hessian-compensated E2M1 rounding, (c) redundant-code remapping, plus (d) ViT distribution-shaping applied BEFORE quantization. All of these keep the on-wire format (E2M1 + per-16 E4M3 + FP32 global=max) and the `torch._scaled_mm_v2` backend unchanged; they only change how `quantize_to_nvfp4` chooses codes/block-scales, or pre-transform tensors.

THREE TIERS.
Calibration-only (no backprop): Four Over Six (per-block 6/4 MSE pick), GPTQ-for-NVFP4 (Hessian weight error-feedback; llm-compressor v0.10 ships it for NVFP4 group-16/E4M3), bias correction (mean output-shift fix), RaZeR (per-block redundant -0 remap to +-5.0), and the ViT activation-shaping pair NoisyQuant + RegCache.
Light-train (few-step block reconstruction): AutoRound/AdaRound-style learned E2M1 rounding (llm-compressor 0.9 AutoRoundModifier has an NVFP4 scheme; 128 samples / 200 iters) and FAAR (FP4-native AdaRound scaling the rounding gradient by the local FP4 interval width; ~4 GPU-hours on a 1B LLM). BRECQ/QDrop are the block-reconstruction wrappers (QDrop's activation-drop is exactly our W4A4 regime).
Heavy (avoid for now): full QAT / QAD and SVDQuant's added low-rank branch (needs a runtime side-GEMM the backend does not have).

VERDICT for our -2.5 pt gap: it is small and almost certainly closable with calibration-only methods. Order: Four Over Six first (cheapest, NVFP4-native, drop-in to `quantize_to_nvfp4`), then GPTQ-for-NVFP4 weight error-feedback, then bias correction (near-free), then ViT shaping (RegCache/NoisyQuant) which targets the exact outlier-token mechanism our skip(2,2) heuristic crudely sidesteps. Only escalate to AutoRound/FAAR light-train if calibration-only stalls above ~0.97 cosine.


## Ranked Recommendations

Ordered highest value / lowest cost first, each mapped to where it plugs into THIS codebase.

1) FOUR OVER SIX (per-block 6-vs-4 max selection) — calibration-only (in fact data-free/weights, MSE-driven). HIGHEST value/cost. Directly fixes our exact code: `quantize_to_nvfp4` hardcodes `amax_b / E2M1_MAX` (always scale to 6), which puts a dead zone between 66.6% and 100% of block max. Quantize each 16-block twice (max->6 and max->4), keep the lower-MSE variant per block. No format change (still E2M1 + E4M3 scale), <15% extra quant cost, no backprop. Reported AWQ Llama-3.1-8B WikiText2 8.33->8.24. For weights it is one-shot offline; for activations it is a per-block MSE pick at calibration time. Effort: ~30 lines in `nvfp4/quant.py` + a per-block selector. arXiv:2512.02010, code github.com/mit-han-lab/fouroversix.

2) GPTQ for NVFP4 (Hessian weight error-feedback) — calibration-only, one-shot, ~128-512 calib images. Replaces the inner RTN of `quantize_to_nvfp4` with column-wise quant + inverse-Hessian compensation onto remaining weights; grid-agnostic, so E2M1 + per-16 E4M3 scale slots straight in. Now production: llm-compressor v0.10 explicitly supports GPTQ on NVFP4 group-16/E4M3. MR-GPTQ numbers: NVFP4 RTN 94.67% -> GPTQ 95.92% recovery on Llama-3.1-8B (and NVFP4+GPTQ > INT4+GPTQ). Two paths: (a) reuse llm-compressor's GPTQModifier with the NVFP4 scheme to quantize the 48 middle Linears, or (b) port the ~Cholesky GPTQ loop calling our E2M1 quantizer. Note for ViT: plain Hadamard rotation HELPS MXFP4 but can HURT NVFP4 under RTN (group-16 already keeps outliers) and only re-helps when coupled with GPTQ — so adopt GPTQ before reaching for rotations. arXiv:2210.17323, 2509.23202.

3) BIAS CORRECTION (mean output-shift) — calibration-only, CHEAPEST in the family (a few forward passes, no Hessian, no training). After quantizing each QuantLinear, measure E[Wx]-E[Q(W)x] on calib batches and fold it into `self.bias`. Near-free, composes with everything above, and our QuantLinear already carries a bias buffer. Do it as the final cleanup pass after 1+2. arXiv:2006.10518.

4) RegCache (prefix register tokens) — calibration-only / training-free, ViT-specific. Curate outlier-prone but semantically-empty register tokens from ~50k reference images, prefix them into the middle (quant-sensitive) blocks to absorb attention-sink/massive-activation outliers that emerge in MIDDLE layers of vision encoders, and drop the internal sink tokens. Format-agnostic, demonstrated stacking on PTQ4ViT/RepQ-ViT/NoisyQuant and evaluated on DINOv2 specifically; largest gains at W4A4. This attacks the same outlier mechanism our skip(2,2) heuristic crudely avoids, but keeps those blocks in FP4. arXiv:2510.04547.

5) RaZeR (redundant-zero remap) — calibration-only. E2M1 wastes a code on -0; per block remap it to a useful extra level (best +-5.0, midpoint of 4 and 6 — exactly our worst-represented region given finding above). Selected per-block by MSE; weights get 4 candidates offline, activations 2 (~2% runtime). Reported 31-35% perplexity-loss reduction, beats Four Over Six + MR-GPTQ on GSM8K. BUT requires the decode path to honor the remapped code, i.e. a tweak to `code_to_value`/`_MAG` plus matching kernel decode — `torch._scaled_mm_v2` uses the standard E2M1 table, so this needs either a custom dequant GEMM or staying in our reference backend. Higher integration cost than 1-4; try after them. arXiv:2501.04052.

6) NoisyQuant (additive noisy bias) — calibration-only, quantizer-agnostic, ViT-specific. Add a fixed uniform bias to activations pre-quant (flattens heavy-tailed post-GELU/fc2 peaks), subtract a denoising bias folded into the next layer's weight. Composes with any format incl. NVFP4. Caveat: published gains are at W6A6/W8A8, not W4A4 — treat as a cheap add-on for the fc2 Linears, not a primary lever. arXiv:2211.16056.

LIGHT-TRAIN tier (escalate only if 1-4 stall above ~0.97 cosine):
7) AutoRound / AdaRound-style learned E2M1 rounding — learns a per-weight round-up/down offset by block-wise output reconstruction; no weight fine-tuning. llm-compressor 0.9 AutoRoundModifier already exposes an NVFP4 scheme (defaults 128 samples / 200 iters / batch 8). Our `block_output_cosines` already gives the per-block target signal and the encoder.layer container for block-wise reconstruction; QDrop's activation-drop (Bernoulli p~0.5) is directly our W4A4 activation-quant regime. arXiv:2004.10568, 2102.05426 (BRECQ), 2203.05740 (QDrop), intel/auto-round.
8) FAAR — FP4-native AdaRound that fixes AdaRound's wrong uniform-grid 0.5 threshold by scaling the rounding gradient by the local FP4 interval width (the right fix for our non-uniform 0.5/1/2 step grid). ~4 GPU-hours on a 1B LLM; our DINOv2-base is far smaller. arXiv:2603.22370.

DO NOT START WITH: SVDQuant (adds a runtime low-rank side-GEMM `_scaled_mm_v2` doesn't provide), full QAT/QAD (heavy; NVFP4-QAD-Report shows it's only needed for small reasoning LLMs at a hard PTQ floor — not our regime), and percentile/MSE GLOBAL-scale search (SP3 proved it clips on NVFP4).


## NVFP4-specific

What is unique to NVFP4's two-level scaling (per-16 E4M3 block + FP32 global=max), and why it reshapes the technique ranking:

1) GLOBAL MUST BE MAX — this is load-bearing and already proven in-repo (SP3). The FP32 global's sole role is to map per-block E4M3 scales into [0,448]; `global = amax/(6*448)`. Any technique that shrinks the global below true amax (percentile, naive activation clipping, or an MSE search that under-shoots) forces high-magnitude blocks' E4M3 scale to saturate at 448 and CLIPS them (SP3: 0.945 -> 0.66 -> 0.41). Consequence: every classic 'clip the global/activation range' calibration recipe is a NEGATIVE-value move here, and the entire value pool shifts to inside-the-block methods. This is exactly why global-scale calibration 'did not help' for us — it was structurally the wrong knob.

2) THE PER-16 BLOCK ALREADY ABSORBS PER-CHANNEL/PER-TOKEN OUTLIERS — the 16-wide block localizes scale so well that AWQ and SmoothQuant (per-channel salient-weight scaling) lose most of their leverage on NVFP4 (NVIDIA's NVFP4-QAD-Report: 'common PTQ algorithms often fail to improve over baseline NVFP4 because the small block size neutralizes traditional outlier mitigation'). So deprioritize AWQ/SmoothQuant vs INT4 intuition.

3) E2M1 ROUNDING IS THE DOMINANT ERROR; E4M3 SCALE-QUANT IS SECONDARY — Four Over Six and SOAR both measure that keeping scales high-precision 'recovers performance completely', i.e. the FP4 element rounding, not the E4M3 scale, is the error. This is why Four Over Six (which only changes the per-block 6-vs-4 scaling choice) and GPTQ/AutoRound (which only change element rounding) are the high-value moves, while heavy scale-optimization (SOAR's closed-form alpha/Delta search) is lower marginal value for us.

4) THE NON-UNIFORM GRID (steps 0.5 in [0,2], 1 in [2,4], 2 in [4,6]) — our `_MID = [..., 2.5, 3.5, 5.0]` shows the worst region is 4-vs-6 (gap of 2, midpoint 5.0). Four Over Six (scale-to-4 densifies near-max), RaZeR (insert +-5.0 exactly in that gap), and FAAR (interval-width-scaled rounding gradient) are all NVFP4-native answers to this specific grid, unlike uniform-grid AdaRound.

5) E4M3 SCALE IS NON-POWER-OF-TWO (vs MXFP4's E8M0) — NVFP4 can place block scales freely, so SOAR's 'decoupled scale search' (high-precision quant-side scale, snap dequant-side to nearest E4M3) and Four Over Six's 6/4 pick are exploitable; MXFP4-only power-of-two tricks do not transfer.

6) BACKEND CONSTRAINT — `torch._scaled_mm_v2` consumes standard E2M1 codes + swizzled E4M3 block scales + FP32 global. Four Over Six, GPTQ, bias-correction, AutoRound/FAAR all produce STANDARD codes/scales -> zero backend change. RaZeR changes the code-to-value table and SVDQuant adds a side-GEMM -> both need a custom dequant path, raising their cost. This is the practical filter that pushes RaZeR below the rounding/scale methods despite its strong LLM numbers.


## ViT-specific

ViT / vision-encoder outlier handling (distinct from LLMs), and how it maps onto our DINOv2 W4A4 NVFP4 setup:

ROOT CAUSES specific to ViTs: (1) post-LayerNorm activations with severe inter-channel magnitude variation; (2) post-GELU activations with an unbounded asymmetric positive tail (the fc2 input); (3) post-Softmax attention scores following a power law (~99% mass near zero, rare large values carry signal); (4) emergent 'massive activation' / attention-sink OUTLIER TOKENS that appear in the MIDDLE layers of vision encoders — unlike LLMs where sinks attach to the first/BOS token. Our `block_output_cosines` monotonic decay through the middle blocks is consistent with (4): error builds exactly where the sink tokens emerge.

DIRECTLY APPLICABLE TO NVFP4 (format-agnostic, compose with our pipeline):
- RegCache (arXiv:2510.04547) is the top ViT pick. Training-free, plug-and-play, evaluated ON DINOv2/CLIP/SigLIP, biggest gains at W4A4. It prefixes curated 'junk' register tokens into the middle blocks to absorb the sink outliers, narrowing dynamic range BEFORE quantization, then deletes the internally-emerging sinks. It attacks the precise mechanism our skip(2,2) heuristic only crudely sidesteps (we keep whole blocks in BF16); RegCache lets those blocks go FP4. Stacks on any PTQ baseline.
- NoisyQuant (arXiv:2211.16056) quantizer-agnostic additive-noise trick that flattens the heavy-tailed post-GELU/fc2 peaks; fold the denoising bias into the next weight. Cheap add-on for fc2 Linears, but its published gains are W6A6/W8A8 — secondary at W4A4.

ADAPTABLE (ideas reusable, mechanics need porting): RepQ-ViT / RepQuant reparameterize per-channel LayerNorm and log-sqrt2 Softmax quantizers into hardware-cheap forms by folding scales into LayerNorm affine params + next-layer weights. These are W4A4-proven on ViTs (RepQuant DeiT-S 75.21%, Swin-B 82.80%) and largely orthogonal to the scaling format, so the 'fold per-channel correction into affine params, keep a richer calibration quantizer than the deployed one' idea adapts to NVFP4 — but much of what they fix (inter-channel LN variation) is ALREADY handled by NVFP4's per-16 block, so marginal value is lower than for INT4. APQ-ViT's blockwise Hessian metric is reusable; its Matthew-effect/twin custom integer-grid quantizers do NOT map onto the fixed E2M1 grid (PTQ4ViT/APQ-ViT marked 'unlikely'/'adaptable').

NET ViT GUIDANCE FOR US: NVFP4's per-16 block already neutralizes the inter-channel/per-token outlier problem that channel-wise quant targets, so the marginal ViT-specific value concentrates on (a) outlier-TOKEN relocation (RegCache — do this) and (b) Softmax-tail handling — but note our attention scores are SDPA->BF16 already, so the Softmax-tail lever is moot in the current design; the live ViT lever is RegCache on the middle-block MLP Linears.


## Open Questions

- Does Four Over Six's per-block 6-vs-4 choice help ACTIVATIONS in our pipeline, or only weights? Our activation block scales are computed on-host then swizzled (PHASE0 finding 4). Adding a per-block 6/4 MSE pick to the online activation quant adds host-side cost per forward — need to measure whether the fidelity gain justifies it for activations vs applying 6/4 to weights only (which is free, offline).
- Will GPTQ actually beat plain RTN on DINOv2 at our scale, or is the gap too small? MR-GPTQ's NVFP4 RTN->GPTQ delta on LLMs was ~1.25 pts recovery; our gap is -2.5 pt k-NN. Need an A/B on the 48 middle Linears before committing to the llm-compressor integration (which also needs verifying its NVFP4 export matches our exact global=amax/(6*448) and SWIZZLE_32_4_4 layout).
- Does RegCache transfer to a HuggingFace-transformers DINOv2 (our model is loaded via transformers, encoder.layer container) given its reference implementation targets specific vision-encoder forward signatures? Prefixing register tokens means editing the forward to inject/drop tokens around the quantized middle blocks — non-trivial vs the clean nn.Linear swap we use now.
- Is the -2.5 pt k-NN drop even worth closing for the target product? SP4 explicitly flags this as a product call. skip(4,4) already gives cosine 0.98 by trading FP4 budget; we should quantify skip(4,4) k-NN before spending on calibration-recovery, since mixed-precision promotion may be cheaper than any technique here.
- Can RaZeR's remapped +-5.0 code be consumed by torch._scaled_mm_v2 at all, or does it strictly require a custom dequant GEMM? If the kernel hard-codes the standard E2M1 table, RaZeR is only usable on our reference backend (no speed) — confirm before investing.
- For AutoRound/FAAR light-train: what is the right reconstruction target on a feature model with no logits/labels? Block-wise output MSE (BRECQ-style) using block_output_cosines as the signal is the natural choice, but we should confirm reconstructing to BF16 block outputs (not a task loss) is sufficient for k-NN feature fidelity.
- Does bias correction interact safely with the per-block scale tricks? If Four Over Six/GPTQ change the quantized weight, bias correction must be computed AFTER them in the pipeline order; need to fix the composition order (FourOverSix -> GPTQ -> bias-correction -> activation calibration).

## Techniques by dimension (raw)

### Calibration-set-driven weight-rounding / reconstruction PTQ and its applicability to NVFP4 (FP4 E2M1 + per-16 E4M3 block scale + FP32 global scale)

The classic calibration-set reconstruction lineage is AdaRound -> BRECQ -> QDrop. AdaRound (Nagel et al., ICML 2020, arXiv:2004.10568) reframes "round up vs round down" as a learnable per-weight decision: it adds a continuous variable to each weight, passes it through a rectified-sigmoid h(.) so the quantized weight is floor(w/s)+h(V), and minimizes a layer-wise output-reconstruction loss ||Wx - hatW x||^2 plus an annealing regularizer that pushes h(V) toward {0,1}. Crucially it derives this from a Taylor/Hessian (second-order) view of the task loss, optimizes only the rounding mask V (one scalar per weight, no fine-tuning of the weights themselves), and needs only ~1024 unlabeled samples and a few minutes-to-an-hour per network. It recovers 4-bit ResNet to within ~1% of FP. BRECQ (Li et al., ICLR 2021, arXiv:2102.05426) keeps AdaRound's learned rounding but reconstructs whole residual/transformer blocks at once instead of single layers, weighting the output error by the Fisher-information (diagonal Hessian) to capture intra-block cross-layer dependency without overfitting; it pushed PTQ to INT2/INT4 (~1024 calibration images, ~240x faster than QAT) and adds Hessian-based mixed precision. QDrop (Wei et al., ICLR 2022, arXiv:2203.05740) adds one idea on top of BRECQ+AdaRound: randomly drop activation quantization (Bernoulli prob p, typically 0.5) during block reconstruction, which flattens the loss surface jointly over calibration and test inputs and is what makes W2A2/W4A4 (activation-quantized) PTQ work. All three are weight-rounding-on-a-grid + small-param reconstruction methods that DON'T require full training.

How they compose with NVFP4 is the key question. NVFP4 stores, per tensor, an FP32 global scale alpha, and per 16-element block an FP8 E4M3 scale Delta_i, and each weight as an E2M1 FP4 code (values {0,0.5,1,1.5,2,3,4,6} with sign). The reconstruction machinery is grid-agnostic: AdaRound/BRECQ/QDrop only need a differentiable quantizer with a fixed grid and a straight-through estimator, so the E2M1 "round up vs round down" decision (e.g. choosing 2 vs 3, or 4 vs 6 after scaling by alpha*Delta_i) is exactly the kind of binary rounding choice these methods learn. This composition is no longer hypothetical: Intel AutoRound (the deployed SignRound v1/v2 descendant of AdaRound; intel/auto-round) does precisely this. It learns a per-weight rounding offset V plus learned clip params alpha,beta via signed gradient descent against block-wise output reconstruction error, and ships experimental MXFP4 and NVFP4 support; in llm-compressor 0.9 it is exposed as AutoRoundModifier with NVFP4/MXFP4 schemes (defaults: 128 samples, 200 iters, batch 8; "best" mode 512 samples / 1000 iters). So AdaRound-style learned rounding of E2M1 codes on a calibration set is real and directly applicable to NVFP4.

The block scales are a separate, newly-active research axis and they compose orthogonally. Because NVFP4's dominant error is the E2M1 rounding (Four Over Six explicitly measured that keeping scales in high precision "recovers performance completely", i.e. E4M3 scale quantization is the smaller error term), the highest-value calibration-driven move is either (a) learn the E2M1 rounding (AutoRound/AdaRound style), or (b) optimize the per-16 block scale Delta_i and global alpha. Recent NVFP4-specific PTQ work targets (b): SOAR (arXiv:2605.12245) does closed-form joint optimization of alpha and Delta_i by minimizing ||W - Q*(alpha*Delta_i)||^2 and adds a Decoupled Scale Search that lets the quant-side scale be high-precision while the dequant-side scale snaps to the nearest E4M3 value (calibration-data-free base method, optionally + GPTQ with 128 WikiText samples, 6-36 min, ~+1.9 pts zero-shot on Qwen3-8B). Four Over Six (arXiv:2512.02010) is a per-block MSE-driven choice between scaling a block to FP4-max 6 vs 4 to dodge the dead zone between 66.6% and 100% of block max (<15% overhead). RaZeR (arXiv:2501.04052) is calibration-driven but operates on the codes, not rounding: it repurposes the redundant FP4 negative-zero and the always-positive E4M3 sign bit to add an extra useful special value per block, lowering per-block error at identical footprint.

Bottom line for NVFP4: (1) AdaRound's learned-rounding mechanism is directly adaptable and already deployed via AutoRound for NVFP4/MXFP4 in llm-compressor; the E2M1 grid is just a non-uniform fixed grid that the soft-rounding/STE machinery handles. (2) BRECQ's block reconstruction and QDrop's activation-drop are also directly adaptable (they are grid-agnostic wrappers around the rounding optimization) and are especially relevant because NVFP4 W4A4 quantizes activations, which is exactly QDrop's regime; AutoRound already does block-wise reconstruction with learned activation clipping. (3) Learning/optimizing the per-16 E4M3 block scales and FP32 global scale on a calibration set is also viable and is what the newest NVFP4-native papers (SOAR, Four Over Six) do, mostly closed-form/MSE rather than gradient-learned, and it composes with learned rounding (orthogonal: rounding picks codes, scale search picks Delta_i/alpha). The main practical caveat is cost/STE quality on the non-uniform E2M1 grid and the discreteness of the E4M3 scale (handled by SOAR's decoupled search), not any fundamental incompatibility.

- **AdaRound (Up or Down? Adaptive Rounding)** [adaptable, calibration-only (~1024 unlabeled samples, minutes; optimizes only the per-weight rounding mask, no backprop into weights)] Per-weight learnable round up/down: quantized w = floor(w/s)+h(V), V optimized by layer-wise output-reconstruction loss + sigmoid-annealing regularizer derived from a 2nd-order (Hessian) Taylor expansion; no weight fine-tuning. — https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.10568
- **BRECQ (Block Reconstruction)** [adaptable, calibration-only (~1024 images, ~240x faster than QAT; optimizes rounding params per block)] Apply AdaRound-style learned rounding but reconstruct whole residual/attention blocks at once, weighting output error by Fisher/diagonal-Hessian to capture intra-block cross-layer dependency; adds Hessian-based mixed precision. Pushed PTQ to INT2/INT4. — https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.05426
- **QDrop** [adaptable, calibration-only (same block-reconstruction budget as BRECQ; only adds stochastic activation masking)] During block reconstruction, randomly drop activation quantization (Bernoulli p~0.5) so the optimizer sees a mix of quantized/full-precision activations; flattens the loss over calib+test data and enables low-bit ACTIVATION quantization (W2A2/W4A4). Sits on top of BRECQ+AdaRound. — https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.05740
- **AutoRound / SignRound (Intel) — deployed AdaRound descendant** [direct, light-train / calibration-only (defaults 128 samples, 200 iters, batch 8; 'best' = 512 samples / 1000 iters; layer-sequential block reconstruction)] Learns per-weight rounding offset V plus learned clip params (alpha,beta) via SIGNED gradient descent against block-wise output reconstruction error; this is the production realization of AdaRound-style learned rounding and ships experimental MXFP4 + NVFP4 support (exposed as AutoRoundModifier in llm-compressor 0.9). — https://github.com/intel/auto-round and https://docs.vllm.ai/projects/llm-compressor/en/latest/examples/autoround/
- **SOAR (Scale Optimization for Accurate Reconstruction)** [direct, calibration-only / light (base method is calibration-data-free closed-form; +GPTQ uses 128 WikiText-2 samples, 6-36 min for 1B-8B, ~15 iters)] NVFP4-native: closed-form joint optimization of FP32 global alpha and per-16 E4M3 block scales Delta_i minimizing ||W - Q*(alpha*Delta_i)||^2 (Closed-form Joint Scale Optimization), plus Decoupled Scale Search letting the quant-side scale stay high-precision while the dequant-side scale snaps to nearest E4M3. Optimizes the SCALES, not the rounding; composes with GPTQ. — https://arxiv.org/html/2605.12245
- **Four Over Six (4/6) Adaptive Block Scaling** [direct, calibration-only / light (<15% overhead; per-block MSE comparison of two scale options, all in register file)] NVFP4-native: per-block, pick whether to scale block max to FP4's 6 or to 4, chosen by per-block MSE, to avoid the dead zone where no FP4 code lands between 66.6% and 100% of block max. Optimizes the per-16 block scale choice (not rounding), calibration/MSE-driven. Reports E4M3 scale quant is a minor error vs FP4 rounding. — https://arxiv.org/html/2512.02010v3 (PDF https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.02010)
- **RaZeR (Redundant Zero Remapping)** [direct, calibration-only / light (per-block special-value selection; same footprint as NVFP4)] NVFP4-native: repurposes the redundant FP4 negative-zero code and the always-positive E4M3 scale sign bit to add one useful per-block special value, reducing per-block quant error at identical memory footprint. Calibration-driven selection of the special value; modifies the CODE SET, not the block scales or rounding-on-grid. — https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.04052 and https://github.com/abdelfattah-lab/NVFP4-RaZeR
- **GPTQ (composes with NVFP4 in llm-compressor)** [direct, calibration-only (~128 samples; one closed-form pass, no gradient descent)] Hessian-based sequential weight error-compensation (not learned rounding); llm-compressor supports applying it to FP4 schemes and SOAR shows it stacks on scale optimization. Included as the dominant deployed calibration-set reconstruction baseline that the rounding methods compete with/complement. — https://docs.vllm.ai/projects/llm-compressor/en/latest/examples/quantization_w4a4_fp4/ and https://github.com/vllm-project/llm-compressor

### Error-compensation PTQ for 4-bit weights (GPTQ/OBQ + bias correction) and its applicability to NVFP4 / FP4 E2M1

Error-compensation PTQ is a family of calibration-driven methods that quantize weights while feeding the induced error back into the still-unquantized weights, using approximate second-order (Hessian) information from a small calibration set. The lineage is: Optimal Brain Surgeon -> Optimal Brain Compression / Optimal Brain Quantizer (OBQ, Frantar & Alistarh, NeurIPS 2022), which quantizes weights one-at-a-time in a greedy saliency order and applies a closed-form Hessian update to the remaining weights; -> GPTQ (Frantar et al., ICLR 2023, arXiv:2210.17323), which fixes the order so the inverse Hessian is computed once and shared, adds lazy block updates and Cholesky reformulation, enabling 4-bit quantization of 175B models in ~4 GPU-hours. Bias correction (Nagel et al.; "Improving Post Training Neural Quantization", Banner et al. arXiv:2006.10518) is a complementary, much cheaper error-compensation step that corrects only the MEAN per-channel output shift E[Wx]-E[Q(W)x] by editing the layer bias term, computed empirically from a handful of calibration batches. These methods are largely format-agnostic in their math: the Hessian/error-feedback update operates on real-valued weight deltas, and only the quantizer Q() at the core of the loop needs to encode the target grid.

The central question -- does GPTQ apply to NVFP4 / FP4 E2M1 rather than only INT4 -- has a clear, recent answer: YES. GPTQ's error-feedback loop is independent of whether the per-weight rounding step snaps to a uniform integer grid or to the non-uniform E2M1 (1 sign, 2 exp, 1 mantissa = 7 positive levels) grid with a per-block FP scale. The quantizer inside the loop simply becomes "round-to-nearest representable NVFP4/MXFP4 value within the group", and the inverse-Hessian compensation still applies. This is now realized in production: llm-compressor v0.10.0 (March 2026) explicitly added GPTQ support for FP4 microscale schemes (NVFP4 group-16/E4M3 scales and MXFP4 group-32/E8M0 scales), reporting that "applying GPTQ to these schemes can result in improved recovery and overall quantization accuracy." The research frontier is MR-GPTQ (Micro-Rotated GPTQ, arXiv:2509.23202, ICLR 2026), which specializes GPTQ to FP4 with MSE-optimized scale grids, static activation reordering, and block-wise fused Hadamard rotations matched to the group granularity.

Reported accuracy recovery is concrete. From MR-GPTQ (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, W4A4, recovery vs FP16): NVFP4 RTN 94.67% -> NVFP4 GPTQ 95.92% -> NVFP4 MR-GPTQ 96.08%; MXFP4 RTN 87.83% -> GPTQ 89.47% -> MR-GPTQ 93.31% (a 3.5pt absolute gain over plain GPTQ on the harder E8M0-scale format). For comparison INT4 RTN 92.63% / GPTQ 92.75%, so NVFP4+GPTQ beats INT4+GPTQ by ~3pt. On Llama-3.3-70B, NVFP4 GPTQ reaches 99.1% and MXFP4 MR-GPTQ 98.4%. A key nuance: Hadamard rotations HELP MXFP4 (heavy-tailed -> Gaussian, less dead-zone underflow) but HURT NVFP4 under plain RTN because group-16 already preserves outliers; the benefit re-appears when coupled with GPTQ-style compensation. NVIDIA TensorRT Model-Optimizer's NVFP4 path instead emphasizes AWQ, SmoothQuant, SVDQuant and AutoQuantize (NVFP4 weights block-16, calibrated global activation scale); GPTQ is supported in ModelOpt's INT4 path and increasingly elsewhere, but the NVIDIA NVFP4 blog foregrounds AWQ rather than GPTQ.

Cost is modest and calibration-only for the whole family. GPTQ/MR-GPTQ are one-shot, no gradient training: MR-GPTQ uses 1024 FineWeb sequences; llm-compressor recipes use ~512 (sometimes as few as 128-256) samples at seq-len 2048; NVIDIA uses 128-512 calibration samples. Bias correction is the cheapest (a few forward passes, no Hessian). Newer error-compensation refinements push recovery further at small extra cost and transfer to FP4 conceptually: GPTAQ (arXiv:2504.02692) makes calibration asymmetric by always matching the quantized layer output to the original FP output (~20 extra lines vs GPTQ); "Rethinking Residual Errors" / Compensation-Aware Error (arXiv:2604.07955, ICLR 2026) adds the intra-layer compensated-vs-original residual for ~5% overhead (Llama2-7B 3-bit C4 ppl 13.60 -> 8.34); "First-Order Error Matters" (arXiv:2507.11017) compensates the first-order term GPTQ's pure second-order view drops. These were demonstrated mostly on INT3/INT4 but their updates are quantizer-agnostic and adaptable to NVFP4. Practical guidance for ViT-NVFP4 work: use llm-compressor's GPTQ+NVFP4 (or MR-GPTQ when activation rotations are acceptable), layer in bias correction as a near-free mean-error fix, and treat AWQ as an alternative/complementary salient-weight scaling rather than a replacement for the Hessian error-feedback loop.

- **OBQ / Optimal Brain Compression (OBC)** [adaptable, calibration-only (one-shot, small calib set; per-layer Hessian inversion, slower than GPTQ due to per-weight reordering)] Greedy one-weight-at-a-time quantization in saliency order with closed-form inverse-Hessian update redistributing each weight's error onto the remaining unquantized weights — https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.11580 (Frantar & Alistarh, NeurIPS 2022); code https://github.com/IST-DASLab/OBC

- **GPTQ for NVFP4 / MXFP4 in llm-compressor** [direct, calibration-only; ~512 samples (down to 128-256) at seq-len 2048; minutes-to-hours one-shot] GPTQModifier with the inner round-to-nearest replaced by E2M1 group quantization (NVFP4 group-16/E4M3, MXFP4 group-32/E8M0); Hessian error feedback unchanged — https://github.com/vllm-project/llm-compressor/releases (v0.10.0, Mar 2026: 'GPTQ now supports FP4 microscale schemes including NVFP4 and MXFP4'); https://docs.vllm.ai/projects/llm-compressor/en/latest/examples/quantization_w4a4_fp4/
- **MR-GPTQ (Micro-Rotated GPTQ)** [direct, calibration-only one-shot; 1024 FineWeb sequences; rotations pre-fused into weights (no runtime cost), light online activation-rotation kernels] FP4-specialized GPTQ: MSE-optimized per-block/per-tensor scale grids, static activation reordering, and weight-fused block-wise Hadamard rotations matched to the FP4 group size — https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.23202 / https://arxiv.org/html/2509.23202v1 (ICLR 2026): NVFP4 GPTQ 95.92% vs RTN 94.67%; MXFP4 MR-GPTQ 93.31% vs GPTQ 89.47% on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
- **Bias correction (mean output-error correction)** [direct, calibration-only, cheapest in family: a few forward passes, no Hessian, no training] After quantization, edit the per-channel layer bias by E[Wx]-E[Q(W)x] measured on calibration data to cancel the systematic mean activation shift — https://arxiv.org/pdf/2006.10518 (Banner/Hubara et al.); Nagel et al. bias-correction (Data-Free Quantization / AdaRound line https://arxiv.org/pdf/2004.10568)
- **GPTAQ (asymmetric calibration)** [adaptable, calibration-only; ~20 extra lines over GPTQ via channel parallelization, neuron decomposition, Cholesky fusion; negligible extra cost] Always match the quantized layer output to the ORIGINAL full-precision output (not the already-compensated weights), propagating preceding-layer output error as a residual to curb error accumulation — https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.02692 (GPTAQ, 2025): LLaMA3.1-405B 4.32 ppl, halves GPTQ's gap on EVA-02
- **Compensation-Aware Error (Rethinking Residual Errors)** [adaptable, calibration-only; ~5% overhead over GPTAQ; minimal extra memory] Fix GPTQ/GPTAQ target misalignment by adding the intra-layer residual between compensated and original weights, strictly aligning to the original FP output — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07955 / https://arxiv.org/html/2604.07955v1 (ICLR 2026): Llama2-7B 3-bit C4 ppl 13.60 -> 8.34

### ViT-specific calibration-based PTQ error reduction (vision transformers / vision encoders)

Vision-transformer PTQ research has converged on a small set of root causes for accuracy loss under low-bit quantization, all distinct from LLMs: (1) post-LayerNorm activations with severe inter-channel magnitude variation (per-channel outliers); (2) post-GELU activations that are highly asymmetric with an unbounded positive tail; (3) post-Softmax attention scores following a power-law where ~99% of mass is near zero but the rare large values carry the attention signal; and (4) emergent "massive activation" / attention-sink outlier tokens that appear in the MIDDLE layers of vision encoders (unlike LLMs where sinks attach to the first/BOS token). The six surveyed methods attack different subsets of these. Two are calibration metric/quantizer-design methods (PTQ4ViT, APQ-ViT), two are reparameterization methods that decouple a high-fidelity calibration quantizer from a hardware-cheap inference quantizer (RepQ-ViT, RepQuant), one is a plug-and-play distribution-shaping trick (NoisyQuant), and one is a training-free structural intervention that relocates outliers via inserted register tokens (RegCache, 2025-26).

On mechanism: PTQ4ViT uses twin uniform quantization (two separate uniform ranges/scales per problematic tensor — one for the negative vs positive halves of post-GELU, one for small-vs-large post-Softmax values) and selects scales with a Hessian-guided (diagonal-Fisher) metric instead of MSE/cosine. APQ-ViT adds a Blockwise Bottom-elimination Calibration (optimizes a block-wise second-order/Hessian metric and prunes the smallest-magnitude error gradients at a gamma-percentile so calibration focuses on output-critical errors) plus Matthew-effect Preserving Quantization (an asymmetric linear quantizer that deliberately spends more bins on the large Softmax values). RepQ-ViT calibrates post-LayerNorm with channel-wise quant and post-Softmax with log-sqrt2 quant, then mathematically reparameterizes these into hardware-friendly layer-wise + log2 quantizers by folding scale factors into LayerNorm affine params and the next layer's weights — a hyperparameter-free, reconstruction-free method. RepQuant extends this with a learnable per-channel DUAL clipping scheme (separate upper/lower Sigmoid-gated clip factors per channel to handle asymmetric outliers) plus GPTQ weight quant, again reparameterized to layer-wise/log2 for inference. NoisyQuant is quantizer-agnostic: it adds a fixed Uniform "noisy bias" N to the activation before quantization and subtracts a denoising bias folded into the next layer's weights after — provably flattening heavy-tailed peaks (post-GELU/fc2 layers benefit most) to reduce error for any given quantizer. RegCache curates outlier-prone but semantically meaningless KV-cache register tokens from reference images, prefixes them into the middle (quantization-sensitive) blocks, and deletes the internally-emerging sink tokens — narrowing the activation dynamic range before any PTQ runs.

Reported accuracy (ImageNet top-1): At W8A8 all methods are near-lossless (<0.5% drop). At W6A6 the field is strong: PTQ4ViT ~78-84%, APQ-ViT and NoisyQuant slightly better, RepQ-ViT/RepQuant ~80-85% (RepQuant within <1% of FP for most models). W4A4 is where they separate sharply — this is the regime most relevant to 4-bit formats. PTQ4ViT and APQ-ViT collapse on harder models (PTQ4ViT DeiT-S 34.08%, ViT-S 42.57%; APQ-ViT DeiT-S 43.55%, ViT-S 47.95%). RepQ-ViT was the first to make W4A4 usable (DeiT-S 69.03%, ViT-S 65.05%, Swin-B 78.32%), and RepQuant pushed further (DeiT-S 75.21%, ViT-S 73.28%, ViT-B 77.84%, Swin-B 82.80% vs FP 85.27%). FQ-ViT essentially fails at W4A4 (0.1%). RegCache is evaluated on modern vision encoders (CLIP/OpenCLIP/SigLIP/DINOv2) rather than ImageNet-trained ViTs and reports large W4A4 gains stacked on top of PTQ4ViT/RepQ-ViT/NoisyQuant/FIMA-Q (e.g., CLIP-B/16 zero-shot W4A4 0.37% to 1.78% on PTQ4ViT, up to +44.51 best-case delta).

Calibration cost: these are all true PTQ — no end-to-end retraining. PTQ4ViT and APQ-ViT use only 32 unlabeled images; PTQ4ViT runs in minutes on one V100, APQ-ViT needs a forward+backward pass per block for Hessian gradients but keeps a ~20% storage footprint. RepQ-ViT is the cheapest: 32 images, NO reconstruction, NO hyperparameters, ~1-3 GPU-minutes. RepQuant uses 1024 images (aligned with GPTQ), with only ~100 Adam iterations per layer to learn dual-clip factors (seconds/layer on an A6000) — no full fine-tuning. NoisyQuant uses 1024 images and a cheap linear search for the noisy-bias parameter. RegCache is training-free: it curates registers from ~50k reference images and does a few rounds of validation search (~1 hour on an RTX 4090 with naive RTN pseudo-quant), then adds negligible inference overhead.

Relevance to NVFP4 (4-bit E2M1 microscaling weights+activations with FP8 per-16-element block scales): the W4A4 regime is the right reference point. Reparameterization methods (RepQ-ViT, RepQuant) target weight/LayerNorm/Softmax tensors and are largely orthogonal to the scaling-format choice, so their core ideas (fold per-channel correction into affine params; keep a richer calibration quantizer than the deployed one) adapt cleanly to NVFP4's block-scaled grid. NoisyQuant is quantizer-agnostic and directly composes with any format including NVFP4. RegCache is fully plug-and-play and format-agnostic (it shapes the distribution before PTQ), making it the most directly applicable — it is already demonstrated stacking on multiple ViT PTQ baselines. PTQ4ViT/APQ-ViT's twin/Matthew-effect quantizers assume custom non-uniform integer grids and map less cleanly onto NVFP4's fixed FP grid, though their Hessian-guided/blockwise calibration metrics are reusable. NVFP4's per-16 micro-blocks already absorb much of the inter-channel/per-token outlier problem that channel-wise quant solves, shifting the marginal value toward Softmax-tail handling and outlier-token relocation (RegCache).

- **PTQ4ViT (twin uniform + Hessian-guided)** [unlikely, calibration-only (32 images, ~3 min on one V100, no fine-tuning)] Two separate uniform quantization ranges/scales per tensor (split negative/positive for post-GELU, small/large for post-Softmax) with scale chosen by a diagonal-Fisher Hessian-guided metric instead of MSE. — https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.12293 (ECCV 2022); W8A8 ViT-B 84.25% (FP 84.54), W6A6 ViT-S 78.63%; W4A4 collapses (DeiT-S 34.08%, ViT-S 42.57% per RepQuant Table I)

- **NoisyQuant (additive noisy bias)** [direct, calibration-only (1024 images, cheap linear search for the bias parameter, no fine-tuning)] Add a fixed Uniform noisy bias to activations before quantization (flattening heavy-tailed peaks) and subtract a denoising bias folded into the next layer's weights after; quantizer-agnostic plug-in proven to reduce error for any quantizer. — https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.16056 (CVPR 2023); W6A6 gains up to +1.7/+1.1/+0.5% for ViT/DeiT/Swin; NoisyQuant-PTQ4ViT W6A6 ViT-S 78.65, DeiT-S 77.43; fc2/post-GELU layers benefit most. Targets W6A6/W8A8 (not W4A4)
- **RegCache (prefix register insertion)** [direct, calibration-only / training-free (curate from ~50k ref images, few validation-search rounds ~1 hr on RTX 4090; negligible inference overhead)] Curate outlier-prone but semantically meaningless KV-cache register tokens from reference images, prefix them into the middle (quantization-sensitive) blocks to absorb attention-sink outliers, and delete internally-emerging sink tokens — narrowing dynamic range before PTQ. — https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04547 (2025-26, vision encoders CLIP/OpenCLIP/SigLIP/DINOv2); plug-in on PTQ4ViT/RepQ-ViT/NoisyQuant/FIMA-Q/ERQ; CLIP-B/16 zero-shot W4A4 on PTQ4ViT 0.37%->1.78%, best-case +44.51 delta, largest gains at 4-bit

### PTQ / calibration-time techniques for reducing quantization error in NVFP4 and MXFP4 (E2M1 4-bit float + block scales)

NVFP4 and MXFP4 share the E2M1 4-bit-float element format but differ in block scaling: MXFP4 uses 32-element blocks with a power-of-two E8M0 scale, while NVFP4 uses 16-element blocks with an E4M3 (FP8, non-power-of-two) per-block scale plus a per-tensor FP32 global scale. The PTQ challenge unique to these formats is twofold: (a) the FP4 grid is *non-uniform* (step sizes 0.5 in [0,2], 1 in [2,4], 2 in [4,6]), so near-maximal values are poorly represented; and (b) NVFP4's tiny 16-wide block already localizes scale so well that classical per-channel outlier-mitigation (AWQ, SmoothQuant) and uniform-grid rounding optimizers (AdaRound) lose much of their leverage. The most NVFP4/MXFP4-specific PTQ wins therefore come from format-aware tricks operating *inside the block*: adaptive per-block max selection (Four Over Six), redundant-zero remapping (RaZeR), and rounding optimizers rebuilt for the irregular grid (FAAR).

Four Over Six (arXiv 2512.02010, MIT/NVIDIA, mit-han-lab/fouroversix) is the headline adaptive per-block scale technique. Each 16-element block is quantized twice — once scaling its max to FP4 value 6 (default), once to 4 — and the variant with lower error is kept per block. For PTQ, MSE selection works best (MAE is preferred for pre-training). Scaling to 4 makes the near-max representable points denser (3 = 75% of block max vs the 4-vs-6 gap), reducing worst-case error on large magnitudes. It is calibration-only, a drop-in for AWQ/GPTQ/RTN/SmoothQuant, does not change the on-wire format (still E2M1 + E4M3 scale), and adds <15% to the NVFP4 quantizer kernel. Reported: Llama-3.1-8B WikiText2 ppl 8.33 to 8.24 with AWQ (BF16 = 7.54), C4 20.69 to 20.34. RaZeR (arXiv 2501.04052) is complementary: E2M1 wastes an encoding on negative zero, so RaZeR per-block remaps that redundant slot to a useful extra level (best is +/-5.0, the midpoint of FP4's two largest values), selected per block by MSE; weights get 4 candidate special values offline, activations 2 with ~2% runtime overhead, yielding 31-35% reduction in perplexity loss and beating both MR-GPTQ and FourOverSix on GSM8K. FAAR (arXiv 2603.22370, Li Auto, Mar 2026) is the AdaRound-for-FP4 answer: standard AdaRound assumes a uniform grid and its fixed 0.5 rounding threshold is wrong for the sparse high-magnitude region of FP4, so FAAR learns continuous per-weight rounding variables through a temperature-sigmoid whose gradient is scaled by the local FP4 interval width; it needs light training (~4 GPU-hours on a 1B model), not pure calibration, and improves Llama3-1B WikiText2 from RTN 14.28 / AdaRound ~13.74 to 12.60.

For rotation- and GPTQ-based recovery, MR-GPTQ inside "Bridging the Gap..." (arXiv 2509.23202) is the strongest published W4A4 PTQ recipe: block-wise Hadamard rotations (k in {16,32,64,128}, Had16 etc.) pre-fused into weights offline with only activations rotated online, GPTQ second-order error compensation operating in the rotated space, and alternating minimization over the per-tensor global scale and per-group scales (NVFP4 exploits its E4M3 non-power-of-two scales; MXFP4 is restricted to power-of-two). It recovers ~96.1% of FP16 on NVFP4 and ~93.3% on MXFP4 for Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, closing the prior 8-10 point gap; it is calibration-only. Note an important divergence in the rotation story: the NVIDIA NVFP4 pretraining recipe argues that NVFP4's hierarchical 16-wide microscaling already absorbs dynamic-range variation, so they *avoid* forward-pass rotation to eliminate FWHT/fusion overhead — whereas llm-compressor's SpinQuantModifier and QuaRot-style recipes still inject (configurable block-size) Hadamard transforms to recover W4A4 accuracy. Stochastic rounding is primarily a *training/backward-pass* device for unbiased gradients (RTN is used in the forward path); MS-EDEN in Quartet II (arXiv 2601.22813) refines this by moving SR noise off the FP4 elements and onto the lower-variance FP8 group scale, giving ~2x lower quantization MSE while staying unbiased — relevant to NVFP4 but a training technique, not a PTQ-calibration one.

Tooling: NVIDIA TensorRT Model Optimizer exposes nvfp4 (max-calibration baseline), nvfp4_mse (MSE/NVFP4MSECalibrator scale search), nvfp4_awq (per-channel scale search), and nvfp4_svdquant (SVD low-rank branch absorbing outliers), plus selective variants (nvfp4_mlp_only, nvfp4_experts_only, nvfp4_omlp_only) for keeping attention/first-last layers in higher precision; 128-512 calibration samples are recommended (~1024 sweet spot). llm-compressor supports W4A4 NVFP4 export to vLLM with optional SpinQuant/QuaRot transforms. On the PTQ-vs-QAD boundary, NVIDIA's QAD report (arXiv 2601.20088 / NVFP4-QAD-Report.pdf, updated Mar 2026) is explicit: PTQ works well for FP8 broadly and for *very large* (100B+) models on NVFP4, but for *small* LLMs and *sensitive/reasoning* tasks the drop is non-negligible — and crucially, "common PTQ algorithms often fail to improve over baseline NVFP4 because the small block size neutralizes traditional outlier mitigation." Concretely, PTQ drops Llama Nemotron Super V1 AIME25 from 46.0% (BF16) to 32.3%, which QAD restores to 45.6%; QAD also recovers AceReason-7B AIME24 (73.0 -> PTQ 69.4 -> QAD 71.7) and is robust to calibration-data quality/coverage. Their deployed recipe keeps attention layers and the first/last two blocks in BF16 and KV-cache in FP8 (where calibration/precision matters most), quantizing the bulk MLP/MoE to NVFP4. Bottom line: calibration-time format-aware methods (Four Over Six, RaZeR, MR-GPTQ, MSE/AWQ calibrators, selective BF16 layers) close most of the gap cheaply, but for small models and reasoning-heavy tasks PTQ has a hard floor that only QAD/QAT crosses.
